Boot sector viruses can be some of the most dangerous for a everyday user. They are hard to remove and
usually require a full system format. A full system will scrub through the hard drive from scratch and
rebuild all of its file structures. This is the case if the virus has indeed encrypted the boot
sector or inordinately damaged the code. In other instances, when hard to notice the virus, a simple
run of an updated antivirus program with a large registry of boot viruses can safely remove the virus.
The boot sector virus was very popular in the 1990's when floppy disks were in high demand. Fortunately,
their frequency has greatly reduce from improvements in BIOS architecture.
